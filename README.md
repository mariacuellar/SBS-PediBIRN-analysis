# Data description

This data was sent to me by Christopher Bryan Brook.

Message from Chris about the data:

> I have 420 cases from the pediBIRN data, which is the data used in
> Hymel et al. and Boos et al. articles. I find the Boos 2022 (attached)
> article interesting, where they do clustering analysis using a Gower
> matrix, and find 2 clusters. The outcomes in Table 1 are, IMO, very
> interesting. Of course, unsupervised clustering then requires
> interpretation and they interpret cluster 1 as AHT, because it has RH
> and SDH etc. But it is cluster 2 that actually has evidence of trauma.
> The interesting part is how severity of intracranial pathology (Loss
> of consciousness with deterioration) and hypoxia dominate Cluster 1.

> I also attach Boos 2025 which is an example of misuse of statistics,
> not just misinterpretation. They use mediator analysis (and
> multivariate analysis) using outcomes (Loss of consciousness and SDH),
> but they label those outcomes as exposures (inertial force) and treat
> them in the analysis as though they were exposures. So they use an
> outcome as a “proxy” for an exposure then treat the outcome as an
> exposure in the analysis. It is shocking and the statistician in the
> team should be ashamed.

> Anyway, what I have been doing is using Latent Class Analysis on the
> data Using 2 classes, I more or less reproduce Boos 2022 clustering
> although the size of split is a bit different the characteristics are
> the same. I then run it with 3 classes and get very interesting result
> where the 3 classes are basically determined by the 3 levels of loss
> of consciousness—1. none, 2. \< 24 hours, 3. \> 24 hours (with or
> without deterioration).

> The rates of RH and SDH then increase between the 3 classes. So this
> can explain the correlation and support the assertion that these are
> just markers of degree of intracranial pathology.

> If you have any experience in LCA, I would love some feedback on what
> I have done. I can send the results and details. I am still in the
> skeptical stage of these results and am trying to see if I missed
> something or did something wrong or if the results are somehow my
> wishful thinking materialising from dodgy analysis…
